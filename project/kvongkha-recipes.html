Chicken Pho

October 19, 2019

Photo by: Global Panorama

Pho is a classic Vietnamese noodle soup bowl. This recipe is for a chicken broth rather than the beef broth you will typically have at a restaurant. My mom used to always make the beef broth, but ever since my Uncle from France visited Seattle and shared his chicken broth recipe, my mom has only made this broth ever since. I do like the chicken broth better because it is more light and usually less salty than the beef. The ingredients list may look intimidating, but pho includes a variety of toppings and condiments. We typically use all of it from bean sprouts to garlic chili oil, but it is up to you! One note to add about this recipe is that the longer the pho broth is boiled, the better the taste and flavor. Enjoy!

Ingredients

Servings: 4-6

  • 1 half or whole chicken as stalk
  • 1 ginger root
  • 1 white or yellow onion
  • 2 shallots
  • 1 cinammon stick
  • dried cardamom
  • star anis
  • green onions
  • cilantro
  • jalapeno
  • basil
  • lime
  • bean sprouts
  • kosher salt
  • sugar or rock sugar
  • fish sauce
  • black pepper or white pepper
  • fresh rice noodles or dried pho noodles

Condiments & Optional

  • beef/pork/chicken meatballs (optional)
  • wonton soup base and chicken bouillion (optional)
  • hoison sauce
  • sriracha
  • garlic chili oil

Steps

  1. Boil a pot of water for the stalk.
  2. Clean chicken and put inside the boiling water with some salt. The chicken should be boiled for about 45 minutes to an hour until it is cooked.
  3. While this is cooking, prepare and bake peeled ginger, cinnamon stick, dried cardamom, star anis, and 1 whole onion (unchopped) for 20 minutes or until there is an aroma.
  4. Place these ingredients from the oven into the boiling pot for aromatics.
  5. Take out any excess unwanted chicken scum and bubbles from the top of the soup for clear broth.
  6. Once the chicken is done cooking, take it out for to be cooled and shredded as a topping with meatballs.
  7. In the pot, add 2 tablespoons of sugar, 2 tablespoons of salt, and 2 tablespoons of fish sauce, and 1 pack of wonton souop base, and chicken bouillion.
  8. Cut meatballs (optional) in half and add to the pho broth.
  9. While the soup is simmering, prepare the noodle bowl.
  10. Wash basil, bean sprouts, onions, lime, and cilantro. These ingredients will be the garnish.
  11. Chop green onions, jalapeno, and cilantro, and slice lime wedges onto a plate for you to add however much you'd like.
  12. Boil a separate small pot of water.
  13. Once the water is boiling, dip fresh rice or dried noodles into water with a strainer for 1 minute and place into a serving soup bowl.
  14. Add all washed and chopped garnishes and condiments such as hoisin sauce, sriracha, and garlic chili oil to your liking.

Thai Panang Curry

October 20, 2019

Photo by: Kurman Communications

Also known as Red Curry, this dish is easy to make as long as you have the right ingredients. My family loves to have our curry over rice. There is the perfect balance of protein and veggies in the dish. What makes this spicy is the curry paste, so based on your liking add as little or as much as you'd like.

Ingredients

Servings: 4-6

  • sliced chicken breast or whole chicken cut into pie slices
  • chopped lemongrass
  • galanga
  • kaifer lime leaves
  • shallots
  • garlic
  • thai basil
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • thai eggplant
  • 1 can of bamboo shoots
  • vegetable/olive oil
  • 1 tsp of kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp of fish sauce
  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • red curry paste

Steps

  1. Clean and cut chicken into slices and set aside in a bowl.
  2. Chop garlic and shallots finely. Then add 1 teaspoon of oil in a pot and add the garlic and shallots. The garlic cooks quickly, so stir for 30 seconds and then add 2 tbsp of red curry paste. You may add more for your preference.
  3. Stir the curry paste so that it does not burn. Gently pour in half of the coconut milk and continue stirring until the paste is mixed in well.
  4. Then pour the rest of the coconut milk into the pot.
  5. Add the chicken along with 1 stalk of chopped lemongrass, galanga, and kaifer lime leaves. The aromatic smell should all be coming together.
  6. Add 1 cup of water and let the chicken cook for 20 minutes.
  7. Wash the veggies: red bell pepper, Thai eggplant, and bamboo shoots. Slice the bell pepper and eggplant. After, place all the veggeis in the pot.
  8. Add 1 tsp of kosher salt, 2 tbsp of fish sauce, and 1 tsp of sugar.
  9. Now that all of the ingredients have been combined, let the curry cook for another 15 minutes to simmer on medium to low heat.
  10. Wash Thai basil leaves and add to the pot. The curry is complete! Enjoy with steamed jasmine rice.

Egg Rolls

October 20, 2019

Photo by: Bryon Lippincott

Are they called egg rolls or spring rolls? On the West Coast, we call them egg rolls when they are fried while spring rolls are wrapped with the clear rice paper and are therefore transparent. Living on the East Coast for three years, I find that the names are interchangable. What do you think? Why do I see egg rolls named as spring rolls? This bite-size, crunchy, and savory appetizer is the perfect dish to bring as a potluck item!

Ingredients

Servings: 10-15

  • 2 lbs of chopped/ground pork/chicken
  • 3 eggs
  • vermicelli noodles
  • grated/finely chopped taro root
  • carrots
  • 1 yellow/white onion
  • green onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • shiitake or black mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp of kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp of soy sauce
  • black pepper
  • white pepper
  • egg roll wrap

Steps

  1. Soak vermicelli noodles into warm water.
  2. Finely slice carrots and mushrooms, dice yellow/white and green onion, and garlic cloves.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, add meat, diced yellow/white onion, diced green onion, and 3 eggs.
  4. Add all sliced veggies: carrots, mushrooms, taro root, and noodles. Mix well with a spoon or spatula.
  5. Add all of the spices: 2 tbsp of kosher salt, 2 tbsp of soy sauce, black pepper, and white pepper. Then mix again!
  6. Once all of the ingedients have been combined, cover the bowl with Saran Wrap and refridgerate it for 15 minutes or longer.
  7. Time is up! Take out our egg roll mix from the fridge. Scoop some of the mixture with a spoon and place it in the middle of the egg roll wrap.
  8. Fold each side tightly and continue to roll the wrap until the end.
  9. Continue to wrap and roll using all of the mixture or you can save it for later.
  10. Meanwhile, in a cooking pot, add vegetable oil just enough to cover the egg rolls once they are placed in the pot.
  11. Place your egg rolls in the oil over medium-high heat. Cook egg rolls for about 15 minutes or until they are golden brown.
  12. Remove from pot and place it over a napkin for the oil to soak. Enjoy with fish sauce!
